Unseen India Tour

Welcome to Incredible INDIA – the place where heaven falls here and a place where you can console your soul, body and mind. Vacations in India: means the world’s most ancient culture and mediaeval architecture; divine rivers and herd, streets; religious rituals, festivals, urban / rural life and much more. Undoubtedly India is prodigious landmass termed a subcontinent is known for its Colourful people, nature, culture, fair and festivals, Forts, Palaces, architecture, Temples, beaches, music, dances, art and humanity to make India the most tremendous country. Indian culture is considered to be amongst the world’s oldest, richest and most diverse culture.

Experience one of the Best INDIAN GOLDEN TRIANGLE TOUR with the and ready to explore the real India : This tour starts and ends in Delhi, India.

This journey is through the most popular tour circuit of India known as the Golden Triangle , which is the combinen of the DELHI , AGRA , JAIPUR to make it different we will visit few more interesting places called ABHANERI a stepwells, Fatehpur Sikri , Choki Dhani , Monkey Temple.

Delhi is a bustling metropolis and the capital region of India, which includes the national capital city, New Delhi. The area around Delhi was probably inhabited before the second millennium BC, and there is evidence of continuous habitation since at least the 6th century BC. The city is believed to be the site of Indraprastha, the legendary capital of the Pandavas in the Indian epic Mahabharata. Through most of its history, Delhi has served as a capital of various kingdoms and empires. It has been captured, sacked and rebuilt several times, particularly during the medieval period, and therefore the modern conurbation of Delhi is a cluster of a number of cities spread across the metropolitan region.

The splendour of Agra – capital of all India under the Mughals – remains undiminished, from the massive fort to the magnificent Taj Mahal. The Taj Mahal is regarded as one of the wonders of the world, and historians have noted that its architectural beauty has never been surpassed. The Taj Mahal was built by a Muslim, Emperor Shah Jahan in the memory of his dear wife and queen Mumtaz Mahal. It is a Mausoleum that houses the grave of queen Mumtaz Mahal at the lower chamber. The Taj Mahal was constructed over a period of twenty-two years, employing twenty thousand workers. It was completed in A.D. 1648.

The Taj Mahal is built entirely of white marble. Its stunning architectural beauty is beyond adequate description, particularly at dawn and sunset. The Taj seems to glow in the light of the full moon. On a foggy morning, the visitors experience the Taj as if suspended when viewed from across the Jamuna river.

Jaipur, also known as ‘Pink City’ was founded by Maharaja Jai Singh II in A. D. 1727. Jaipur is famous for its rich cultural heritage and has today become the most popular tourist destination in India. Here one gets to see the architectural marvels of ancient forts and monuments that stand tall to this day. The city has acquired the name Pink City because of the extensive use of pink color on the walls during the visit of the Prince of Wales. The city is garlanded by the presence of a number of beautiful monuments that echo the splendid history of the city. It is no surprise then that tourists coming to this city always feel the urge to come back.

Detailed Tour Itinerary

Day 01: Arrive Delhi
Upon your arrival at Delhi International Airport. A representative of our company will meet you at Airport and after a warm welcome transfer to hotel. Evening free for leisure and overnight in Hotel.
Day 02: Delhi Sightseeing
Morning visit the of Old Delhi –Visit Raj Ghat, a simple memorial to Mahatma Gandhi, drive past the Red Fort continuing to the Jama Masjid by walk and it is the ultimate place for photographers where you can capture the amazing pictures of this crowded area, and later visit the spice market . The tour continues to Jama Masjid, one of Asia’s largest mosques and which is viewed from the outside. drive pass out side through The Red Fort, President’s house and the Secretariat buildings, India Gate – The War Memorial, Humanyu Tomb & Qutub Minar and after exploring the beauty of Delhi , back to hotel
Day 03: Samode – Jaipur ( 01hrs drive / 40kms )
Morning after breakfast straight drive to Samode Palace, upon your arrival check in & evening free for leisure and also go for a village walk. Samode: Samode is a small but beautiful town of Rajasthan. It is situated 40 km west of Jaipur - the capital of Rajasthan. It is an ideal spot to spend one's holidays in memorable way. Deep in the desert, against a background of stony hills, Samode is globally famous for its Samode Palace. Overnight in this royal hotel.
Day 4: Samode – Jaipur ( 01hrs drive / 40kms )
Morning after breakfast straight drive to the pink city Jaipur, on arrival visit to Amber Fort, the ancient Capital of the state until 1728. Enjoy the Elephant ride up to the hill on which the Fort is situated. And do the ultimate Photography while climbing the fort on Elephants Visit the Jagmandir or the hall of victory, Jaimahal (Look at the beautiful mirror work and intricate inlay work) Later city sightseeing tour visiting Maharaja’s City Palace and the Observatory. Drive pass Hawa Mahal or the Palace of winds through the rose – pink residential and business areas of Jaipur and time to photography and also visit the water palace and do the street photography. Evening option to visit the Choki Dhani (about 30kms from Jaipur ) to get the ultimate experience of real Rajasthani culture and food. Dinner at Choki Dhani and overnight in Hotel.
Day 05: Jaipur –Pushkar - Khimsar
Morning after breakfast straight drive to Khimsar Enroute visit the Pushkar city, On your arrival in Khimsar check-into the Khimsar Fort . evening free for leisure or independent activities and overnight at Fort, This magnificent fort is literally off the beaten track. Set in the midst of sand dunes, this amazing fort stands as securely as it did over many centuries of battle .
Day 06:Khimsar – Jodhpur (drive about 02hrs / 100kms )
Morning after breakfast straight drive to the blue city – Jodhpur, on arrival visit the imposing Mehrangarh Fort with its sprawling courtyards and intricately carved balconies. Close to the fort is the 19th century royal cenotaph, Jaswant Thada, a creation in white marble which displays some rare portraits of Jodhpur's rulers. Evening free to stroll in local bazaar / market and time for photography, Overnight in Jodhpur.
Day 07: Jodhpur – Deogarh (150kms / 03hrs drive )
Morning after breakfast option to go for Bishnoi Village Jeep safari, we will visit the Salawas which is situated on the outskirts of Jodhpur, around 20 Kilometers away from the heritage city which is known for its vibrancy and culture. To get the contemporary dwelling and a mesmerizing experience to our guests that truly match the real purpose of tourists’ visit in Jodhpur, later continue drive to Deogarh, on arrival transfer to hotel. Evening explore this beautiful hotel and enjoy the culture Rajasthani Cuisine Food with Folk dance and overnight at Deogarh.
Day 08:Deogarh – Ranakpur – Udaipur
Morning after breakfast option to go for village Jeep Safari and later continue drive to the city of Lakes Udaipur, Udaipur Enroute visit the stunning Ranakpur Temples. Built in the 15th century, the Ranakpur temples are very well preserved and one of the most holy places for Jain pilgrimage. These temples lie in a remote and peaceful valley of the Aravali range. The main temple, dating back to 1439, is called the Chaumukha Temple or the “Four-faced temple” and is dedicated to Adinath; the first of Jain teachers. We have lunch after our visit and continue to Udaipur arriving in the early evening. Transfer to your hotel. Overnight in Hotel.
Day 9: Udaipur
Morning after breakfast visit the romantic city of Udaipur. Visit the City Palace which is the largest palace complex in Rajasthan; Jagdish temple, a fine Indo Aryan temple built in 1651; Saheliyon ki bari - boat ride at Lake Pichola and transfer to hotel. Evening free for leisure and overnight at hotel.
Day 10: Udaipur – Chittorgarh - Bundi (drive about 06hrs / 265kms )
Morning after breakfast straight drive to Bundi, enroute visit the Chittorgarh Fort, and after exploring the Chittorgarh continue drive towards Bundi is one of the most of the popular tourist places in Rajasthan. Blessed with an unending natural beauty, the particular place is great to visit as it boasts a perfect combination of natural beauty and historical significance. Hence, tourists of all kinds get a great experience by visiting the places. And overnight in Hotel.
Day 11: Bundi – Talabgaon (200kms / drive about 05hrs )
Morning after breakfast drive to Talabgaon, Escape to the untouched surrounds of Talabgaon and experience the real Indian countryside on arrival transfer to Talabgaon Castle – a 200 year old, beautifully restored Fort nestled in the palm of rustic nature – now re-imagined as a Heritage Resort for all to experience. Afternoon option to experience the Camel ride / Horse safari or village Jeep Safari and evening attend the Culture puppet show or folk dance at Castle with the Rajasthani Traditional dinner. Overnight at Hotel
Day 12: Talabgaon – Bharatpur – Fatehpur Sikri – Agra
Morning after breakfast straight drive to Agra, Enroute visit the Rickshaw ride at Bharatpur in search of Birds and later continue drive toward Agra , Enroute visit the city of Mughal emperor – Fatehpur Sikri(40 KMS from Agra) built by Emperor Akbar in 1569 and abandoned it just after 15 years due to the scarcity of water. Visit the graceful buildings – The punch Mahal, Anup Talab,Diwan-e- Khas, Tomb of the holy saint Salim Chisti, Buland Darwaza and other important palaces where one experiences not only the Architectural brilliance but also its perfect fusion. After Fatehpur Sikri drive to Agra and overnight in hotel.
Day 13: Agra – Delhi – (drive about 04hrs )
Early Morning visit the sunrise view of famous Mausoleum – The Taj Mahal, an unique epitome of Love built by Moghul Emperor Shahjahan in 1630 for his queen Mumtaz Mahal to enshrine her mortal remains. A Sahajahan’s drop of tear on the cheek of Time that has now crystallized. After exploring the beauty of Taj Mahal transfer to Hotel for fresh up and later visit the Red Fort , after exploring the beauty of Taj drive back to Delhi. Overnight in Hotel.

Please note: Taj Mahal is closed on Every Friday.

Day 14: Delhi - Varanasi ( by Fly ) – Sarnath – Varanasi.
Morning after breakfast transfer to Airport to catch the morning flight to the city of Ganges Varanasi, upon your arrival transfer to Hotel and later visit the Sarnath, one of the most holy places for all Buddhists. It is believed that in Sarnath Buddha gave his first sermon to his disciples after achieving enlightenment. It is a place of pilgrimages from all over the world. Many Asian countries have built Buddhist temples there following their own ancient architectural traditions. Sarnath is located 15 km from Varanasi, evening go to the Ghats via Rickshaw ride and witness the most spiritual aarti ceremony of Hindus, overnight at hotel.
Day 15: Varanasi – Delhi ( by Fly )
Early in the morning we will go again to the Ganges River to observe (from the boat) the Hindu believers starting their day bathing in the river. For each Hindu it is an essential activity symbolizing the greeting of the sun as it rises above the holy river of the Gods. And drive back to Hotel after breakfast transfer to Airport to catch the flight for Delhi. On Arrival transfer to your hotel. Evening free for leisure and overnight at hotel.
Day 16: Delhi – Departure.
Morning after breakfast transfer to Airport as per your flight timing to catch the flight for onward destinations.
