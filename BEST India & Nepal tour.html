BEST India & Nepal tour

Welcome to Incredible INDIA – the place where heaven falls here and a place where you can console your soul, body and mind. Vacations in India: means the world’s most ancient culture and mediaeval architecture; divine rivers and herd, streets; religious rituals, festivals, urban / rural life and much more. Undoubtedly India is prodigious landmass termed a subcontinent is known for its Colourful people, nature, culture, fair and festivals, Forts, Palaces, architecture, Temples, beaches, music, dances, art and humanity to make India the most tremendous country. Indian culture is considered to be amongst the world’s oldest, richest and most diverse culture.

Experience one of the Best INDIAN GOLDEN TRIANGLE TOUR with the and ready to explore the real India : This tour starts and ends in Delhi, India.

This journey is through the most popular tour circuit of India known as the Golden Triangle , which is the combinen of the DELHI , AGRA , JAIPUR to make it different we will visit few more interesting places called ABHANERI a stepwells, Fatehpur Sikri , Choki Dhani , Monkey Temple.

Delhi is a bustling metropolis and the capital region of India, which includes the national capital city, New Delhi. The area around Delhi was probably inhabited before the second millennium BC, and there is evidence of continuous habitation since at least the 6th century BC. The city is believed to be the site of Indraprastha, the legendary capital of the Pandavas in the Indian epic Mahabharata. Through most of its history, Delhi has served as a capital of various kingdoms and empires. It has been captured, sacked and rebuilt several times, particularly during the medieval period, and therefore the modern conurbation of Delhi is a cluster of a number of cities spread across the metropolitan region.

The splendour of Agra – capital of all India under the Mughals – remains undiminished, from the massive fort to the magnificent Taj Mahal. The Taj Mahal is regarded as one of the wonders of the world, and historians have noted that its architectural beauty has never been surpassed. The Taj Mahal was built by a Muslim, Emperor Shah Jahan in the memory of his dear wife and queen Mumtaz Mahal. It is a Mausoleum that houses the grave of queen Mumtaz Mahal at the lower chamber. The Taj Mahal was constructed over a period of twenty-two years, employing twenty thousand workers. It was completed in A.D. 1648.

The Taj Mahal is built entirely of white marble. Its stunning architectural beauty is beyond adequate description, particularly at dawn and sunset. The Taj seems to glow in the light of the full moon. On a foggy morning, the visitors experience the Taj as if suspended when viewed from across the Jamuna river.

Jaipur, also known as ‘Pink City’ was founded by Maharaja Jai Singh II in A. D. 1727. Jaipur is famous for its rich cultural heritage and has today become the most popular tourist destination in India. Here one gets to see the architectural marvels of ancient forts and monuments that stand tall to this day. The city has acquired the name Pink City because of the extensive use of pink color on the walls during the visit of the Prince of Wales. The city is garlanded by the presence of a number of beautiful monuments that echo the splendid history of the city. It is no surprise then that tourists coming to this city always feel the urge to come back.

Detailed Tour Itinerary

DAY 01 : Arrive at Mumbai Airport + Sightseeing….

Tour Starts :

Our Driver and Guide will meet you at your hotel Golden Swant about 08hrs… and then you can start the Mumbai Tour Sightseeing.

Things to Experience

  • Dhobhi Ghat
  • Meet the lunch boxes man
  • Gandhi’s Homes
  • Victorian Gothic architecture, railway station
  • Gateway of India
  • Juhu Beach
  • Marine Drive
DAY 02:Mumbai – Aurangabad - BY FLY
Upon your arrival meet and greet with our Representative and Guide at Airport, on arrival straight drive towards the Ajanta Caves. the Ajanta Caves- is one of the world's greatest historical monuments, recognized by UNESCO, located in India. There are 30 caves that were discovered in AD 1819. The first Buddhist cave monuments at Ajanta date from the 2nd and 1st centuries B.C. All of the paintings show heavy religious influence and center around Buddha, Bodhisattvas, and incidents from the life of Buddha and the Jatakas. The Ajanta caves preserve some of the best masterpieces of Buddhist art in India. Later continue drive towards the Aurangabad.

PS: AJANTA caves are closed on Every Monday & ELLORA caves are closed on every Tuesday.


DAY 03:Aurangbad – Mumbai - By FLY
Moring after breakfast straight drive to Ellora caves, about 35kms from Aurangabad.

Enroute option to visit the Daulatabad Fort, if you are keen with the Indian History, then you will definitely like this unpopular Fort, famous for its formidable hill fort. The fort is situated on an isolated cone-shaped hill rising abruptly from the plain to the height of about 190 metres. The fortification constitutes of three concentric lines of defensive walls with large number of bastions.

Ellora Caves (about 40kms from Aurangabad)

One of the most wondrous and breathtaking places in India: the Incredible Indian Cave Temples. The 34 monasteries and temples, extending over more than 2 km, were dug side-by-side in the wall of a high basalt cliff. Ellora, with its uninterrupted sequence of monuments traditionally dated from A.D. 600 to 1000, brings the civilization of ancient India to life. Not only is the Ellora complex a unique artistic creation and a technological marvel, but also with its sanctuaries devoted to Buddhism, Hinduism, and Jainism, it illustrates the spirit of tolerance that was characteristic of ancient India.
After exploring the beauty of Ellora caves, transfer to Airport to catch the flight for Mumbai, upon your arrival at airport, transfer to hotel Golden Swan.
DAY 04:Mumbai – Udaipur ( by Fly)
Early Morning transfer to Airport, to catch the flight for Udaipur, upon your arrival at Udaipur our driver will meet you at Airport with the displaying board . Morning pickup from Airport and transfer to hotel for fresh up and after breakfast, continue the sightseeing tour of Udaipur, Visit the City Palace which is the largest palace complex in Rajasthan; Jagdish temple, a fine Indo Aryan temple built in 1651; Saheliyon ki bari - boat ride at Lake Pichola and transfer to hotel. Evening free for leisure overnight at hotel.
DAY 05:Udaipur – Ranakpur – Jodhpur
Morning after breakfast straight drive to the Blue City – Jodhpur. Enroute visit the stunning Ranakpur Temples. Built in the 15th century, the Ranakpur Temples are very well preserved and one of the most holy places for Jain pilgrimage. These temples lie in a remote and peaceful valley of the Aravali range. The main temple, dating back to 1439, is called the Chaumukha Temple or the “Four-faced temple” and is dedicated to Adinath; the first of Jain teachers. We have lunch after our visit and continue to Jodhpur, evening free to stroll in local bazaar / Market and overnight in Hotel.
DAY 06:Jodhpur Sightseeing – Jaipur (drive about 06hrs)
Morning after breakfast visit the imposing Mehrangarh Fort with its sprawling courtyards and intricately carved balconies. Close to the fort is the 19th century royal cenotaph, Jaswant Thada, a creation in white marble which displays some rare portraits of Jodhpur's rulers. Later continue drive towards the the Pink City Jaipur, upon your arrival evening free for leisure and overnight at hotel.
DAY 07:Jaipur Sightseeing.
Morning at 7:30hrs visit Amber Fort, the ancient Capitol of the state until 1728. Enjoy the Elephant ride up the hill track, on which the Fort is situated. Enjoy the beautiful mirror work and intricate inlay work at Jai Mandir (Hall of Victory)!

Later we are on a city sightseeing tour, visiting Maharaja’s City Palace and the Observatory. Drive past Hawa Mahal or the Palace of winds through the ‘rose – pink’ residential and business areas of Jaipur.Take time forstreet photography

In the evening, we drive you to Choki Dhani (about 30kms from Jaipur) to get the ultimate experience of the Rajasthani culture and food. Dinner is at Choki Dhani and overnight in Hotel (TBA).
DAY 08:Jaipur – Ranthambore (drive about 03hrs )
Early Morning after breakfast straight drive to Ranthambore, on arrival get ready for the Wild Jungle Jeep safari in search of Tigers, If luck persist : you can see the Tiger and other wild animals. Overnight at hotel.
DAY 09:Ranthambore - Agra
Early Morning once again get ready to experience the Jungle Jeep Safari and later continue drive towards the Agra, On arrival visit the Evening Sunset view of the Taj Mahal - an unique epitome of Love built byMoghul Emperor Shahjahan in 1630 for his queen Mumtaz Mahal to enshrine her mortal remains. A Sahajahan’s drop of tear on the cheek of Time that has now crystallized. If time permit: Then visit the Agra Fort, else you can capture the picture from outside and overnight at hotel.
DAY 10:Agra – Jhansi ( by Train ) – Orchha – Khajuraho
Early Morning once again option to visit the Sunrise view of Taj Mahal and later transfer to Agra Railway Station to catch the Train for Jhansi ( about 10kms from Orchha ) , once again our driver will meet you at Railway Station with the the displaying board i.e. On arrival enjoy the lunch in heritage restraunt and visit the Orchha fort, later continue drive towards to Khajuraho. On arrival transfer & overnight at hotel.
DAY 11:Khajuraho – Varanasi
Early Morning after breakfast visit the Khajuraho visit the most important and fascinating western group of temples. The temples of Khajuraho have been declared a 'world heritage' site by UNESCO in order to preserve the rich legacy of architecture, bequeathed to future generations by the Kings of the Chandela dynasty. These temples are famous for their intricate sculptures and erotic carvings that are one of the outstanding achievements of Indian art.Afternoon transfer to Airport to catch the flight for Varanasi, upon your arrival at Airport, our Representative will meet you and transfer to your hotel. Evening attend the Aarti Ceremony on the Ghats and overnight at hotel.
DAY 12: Varanasi Sightseeing.
This morning an early start for a dawn boat cruise on the Ganges river. Ritual is a very important aspect of Hinduism and there is no better place to observe it than at the Ganges. You will view the thread ceremony, head shaving and food being offered to the departing souls. Also along its bank witness devotees bathing, cremating their dead and doing laundry . A walk through narrow twisting lanes will give you a chance to witness the various rituals o f the pilgrims.
Morning sightseeing tour of Varanasi: the eternal city. The most scared pilgrim centre in India and a major tourist attraction. An ancient centre of learning and Afternoon excursion to Sarnath (9 kms) an important pilgrimage centre for Buddhist. This is where the Buddha preached his first sermon after getting enlightenment. The city is famous for its magnificent stupas, monasteries and temples Evening free for leisure. Overnight at hotel.
DAY 13:Varanasi – Kathmandu
Morning breakfast check out from Hotel and transfer to Airport to catch the flight for Kathmandu, start sightseeing tour of Patan city. And overnight at hotel.
Just outside the arrival gate so, please ask them to look for their name carefully once they get out of the arrival gate.
DAY 14:Kathmandu
Morning after breakfast Full day sightseeing tour of Bhaktapur, Pashupatinath, Boudhanath and Nagarkot and overnight at hotel
DAY 15:: Kathmandu - Delhi
Morning Half day sightseeing tour of Kathmandu Durbar Square and later transfer to Airport to catch the flight for Delhi, on arrival transfer & overnight at hotel.After a good breakfast, you will first visit Old Delhi –Visit Raj Ghat, a simple memorial to Mahatma Gandhi. Drive past the Red Fort, continuing to Jama Masjid by walk.It is the ultimate place for photographers, providing ample opportunities to capture the essence of the place! You would visit the spice market, taking in the colour and aroma. We reach Jama Masjid, one of Asia’s largest mosques. This can only be viewed from the outside. We then drive by The Red Fort, President’s House, the Secretariat buildings and India Gate – The War Memorial.We explore the Humayun Tomb & Qutub Minar. After drinking in the beauty of Delhi, we are back at the Hotel (TBA)
DAY 16:Delhi – Free day for leisure.
Monrnig free day for leisure or independent activities.
DAY 17:Departure. – Tansfer to Airport Morning.
Early Morning transfer to Airport to catch the flight for Home Country
