South Ancient India Tour

Welcome to Incredible INDIA – the place where heaven falls here and a place where you can console your soul, body and mind. Vacations in India: means the world’s most ancient culture and mediaeval architecture; divine rivers and herd, streets; religious rituals, festivals, urban / rural life and much more. Undoubtedly India is prodigious landmass termed a subcontinent is known for its Colourful people, nature, culture, fair and festivals, Forts, Palaces, architecture, Temples, beaches, music, dances, art and humanity to make India the most tremendous country. Indian culture is considered to be amongst the world’s oldest, richest and most diverse culture.

Experience one of the Best INDIAN GOLDEN TRIANGLE TOUR with the and ready to explore the real India : This tour starts and ends in Delhi, India.

This journey is through the most popular tour circuit of India known as the Golden Triangle , which is the combinen of the DELHI , AGRA , JAIPUR to make it different we will visit few more interesting places called ABHANERI a stepwells, Fatehpur Sikri , Choki Dhani , Monkey Temple.

Delhi is a bustling metropolis and the capital region of India, which includes the national capital city, New Delhi. The area around Delhi was probably inhabited before the second millennium BC, and there is evidence of continuous habitation since at least the 6th century BC. The city is believed to be the site of Indraprastha, the legendary capital of the Pandavas in the Indian epic Mahabharata. Through most of its history, Delhi has served as a capital of various kingdoms and empires. It has been captured, sacked and rebuilt several times, particularly during the medieval period, and therefore the modern conurbation of Delhi is a cluster of a number of cities spread across the metropolitan region.

The splendour of Agra – capital of all India under the Mughals – remains undiminished, from the massive fort to the magnificent Taj Mahal. The Taj Mahal is regarded as one of the wonders of the world, and historians have noted that its architectural beauty has never been surpassed. The Taj Mahal was built by a Muslim, Emperor Shah Jahan in the memory of his dear wife and queen Mumtaz Mahal. It is a Mausoleum that houses the grave of queen Mumtaz Mahal at the lower chamber. The Taj Mahal was constructed over a period of twenty-two years, employing twenty thousand workers. It was completed in A.D. 1648.

The Taj Mahal is built entirely of white marble. Its stunning architectural beauty is beyond adequate description, particularly at dawn and sunset. The Taj seems to glow in the light of the full moon. On a foggy morning, the visitors experience the Taj as if suspended when viewed from across the Jamuna river.

Jaipur, also known as ‘Pink City’ was founded by Maharaja Jai Singh II in A. D. 1727. Jaipur is famous for its rich cultural heritage and has today become the most popular tourist destination in India. Here one gets to see the architectural marvels of ancient forts and monuments that stand tall to this day. The city has acquired the name Pink City because of the extensive use of pink color on the walls during the visit of the Prince of Wales. The city is garlanded by the presence of a number of beautiful monuments that echo the splendid history of the city. It is no surprise then that tourists coming to this city always feel the urge to come back.

Detailed Tour Itinerary

DAY 01 : Chennai – Tamilnadu

Welcome to Tamilnadu state. Language spoken in this state is Tamil.

Airport transfer, check-in and relax.

Enjoy the BBQ or South Indian dinner and go for a walk by the beach.

DAY 02:Chennai – Tamil nadu – Mamallapuram – Tamilnadu

Wake up late, visit Madras Government Museum, an excellent Bronze Sculpture Gallery and Stone Sculpture Gallery as well as an excellent coin collection (coins are also an integral part of archaeology in India). Enjoy the South Indian lunch and head to Mamallapuram.

You will stun at the monuments which are mostly rock-cut and monolithic, and constitute the early stages of Dravidian architecture where in Buddhist elements of design are prominently visible. They are constituted by cave temples, monolithic rathas (chariots), sculpted reliefs and structural temples. The pillars are of the Dravidian order. The sculptures are excellent examples of Pallava art. They are located in the side of the cliffs near India's Bay of Bengal. Enjoy the sunset and check-in into the hotel. Refresh quickly for a cultural show which reflects the culture of the state and the cocktail dinner.

DAY 03:Mamallapuram – Arikamedu – Pondicherry – Kanchipuram – Tamilnadu

Wake up early to ensure you do not miss the sunrise. Finish breakfast and leave to the next destination.

One of the best Roman sites. This is located at a distance 7 km from its capital Pondicherry, a union territory. Arikamedu was an important bead making centre.Arikamedu is known to have been inhabited since before the 1st century CE and was more or less continually occupied until modern times.Packed lunch will be served as you need to reach Kanchipuram at the earliest. We ensure, you get a relaxed dinner.

In Hindu theology, Kanchipuram is one of the seven Indian cities to reach final attainment.Of the 108 holy temples of the Hindu god Vishnu, 14 are located in Kanchipuram.Kanchipuram has been chosen as one of the heritage cities for HRIDAY - Heritage City Development and Augmentation Yojana scheme of Government of India.The city is well known for its hand woven silk sarees and most of the city's workforce is involved in the weaving industry.You will stay overnight in Kanchipuram after the dinner.

DAY 04:Kanchipuram – Tamilnadu -Chennai – Tamilnadu -Cochin – Kerala

Leave Kanchipuram depends on your flight timing from Chennai to Cochin

You will be dropped at the airport

Welcome to Kerala state. Language spoken in this state is Malayalam.Airport transfer, check-in and relax.Enjoy the Kerala special dinner.

DAY 05:Cochin – Kerala
Pattanam excavation, site for archaeological research at Pattanam covers about 45 hectares. Due to habitation activities it is a “disturbed” site; some parts are partially destroyed due to sand quarrying. The site seems to have been first occupied by indigenous population around 1000 BC and continued to be active till the 10th century AD.Square copper coins (on one side an elephant and on the other bows and arrows) had been found at the site. These types of coins were issued in the beginning of Christian era.

You will end the day with a cultural show and dinner at Greenix village.

DAY 06:Cochin – Kerala - Bangalore – Karnataka

After breakfast, you can do a quick visit to Kerala Folklore museum, a cultural hub to promote and preserve the culture & heritage of South India. Then you will be dropped at the airport to go to the next destination.

Welcome to Karnataka state. Language spoken in this state is Kannada.Airport transfer, check-in and relax.Get ready for your dinner at one of the local pubs. Hit the bed early as you will have to wake up early the next day.

DAY 07:Halebid – Karnataka
Wake up and leave early with packed breakfast to Halebid, home to some of the best examples of Hoysala architecture.The temple complex comprises two Hindu temples, the Hoysaleswara and Kedareswara temples and two Jain basadi. The two Nandi images on the sides of the Hoysaleswara temple are monoliths.The Hoysaleswara temple, dating back to the 1121 C.E., is astounding for its wealth of sculptural details. The walls of the temple are covered with an endless variety of depictions from Hindu mythology, animals, birds and shilabalikas or dancing figures. No two sculptures of the temple are the same. This temple, guarded by a Nandi bull, was never completed, despite 86 years of labour.
DAY 08:Bangalore – Karnataka – Hyderabad– Telungana
You will be dropped at the airport after breakfast.

Welcome to Telungana state. Language spoken in this state is Telugu.

Airport transfer, check-in and relax.

You will enjoy Biriyani (an Indian dish made with highly seasoned rice and meat, fish, or vegetables) for dinner

DAY 09:Hyderabad - Telungana – Warangal– Telungana
Wake up early and leave to Warangal after breakfast
Warangal is known for its heritage. "Oru" terms the word 'single', "Kallu" is referred as a 'stone'. The word "Orugallu" synonyms, one rock, which describes the city was carved out of a single large stone.The Kakatiyas left many monuments, including an impressive fortress, four massive stone gateways, the Swayambhu temple dedicated to Shiva, and the Ramappa temple situated near Ramappa Lake. You will stay overnight in this city.
DAY 10:Nagarjunakonda – Andhra Pradesh – Hyderabad - Telungana

Welcome to Andhra Pradesh state. Language spoken in this state is Telugu.

Get ready to explore the historical Buddhist town. It is one of India's richest Buddhist sites, known in the ancient times as Sri Parvata.During the early centuries AD, the site housed more than 30 Buddhist viharas; excavations have yielded art works and inscriptions of great significance for the scholarly study of the history of this early period.The great stupa at Nagarjunakonda belongs to the class of uncased stupas, its brickwork being plastered over and the stupa decorated by a large garland-ornament.


You will return to Hyderabad for overnight stay and dinner.

DAY 11:Hyderabad– Telungana – Bhubaneshwar – Odisha

You will be dropped at the airport after breakfast.


Welcome to Odisha state. Language spoken in this state is Oriya.Hindi, Urdu, Bengali and Telugu are widely understood and sometimes spoken.

Airport transfer, check-in and relax.

Delicious local cuisine will be served for dinner.

DAY 12:Sisupalgarh – Odisha - Konark – Odisha
Wake up early to visit Sisupalgarh, a ruined fortification. It is one of the largest and best preserved early historic fortification in India. The major portion of the land that constitutes the ancient city is in private possession hindering archaeological study. Since the site is nationally protected, it lies within the bailiwick of the Archaeological Survey of India.

Post lunch, you will visit Konark, the site of the 13th-century Sun Temple, also known as the Black Pagoda, built in black granite. The temple is a World Heritage Site. The temple is now mostly in ruins, and a collection of its sculptures is housed in the Sun Temple Museum, which is run by the Archaeological Survey of India.The Sun Temple was built in the 13th century and designed as a gigantic chariot of the Sun God, Surya, with twelve pairs of ornamented wheels pulled by seven horses. Some of the wheels are 3 meters wide.

DAY 13:Konark – Odisha – Jaugada (Ganjam) – Odisha

You will leave to Jaugada after your breakfast

You will be visiting Jaugada, a ruined fortress. Jaugada stands as the symbol of all the historical heritage connected with the Rushikulya Valley civilisation.The inscription of this place is one of the two Kalinga edicts which are supplements to the series of fourteen rock edicts of Ashoka, the other being at Dhauli near Bhubaneswar in Puri district. These two Kalinga edicts laid down the principles on which the newly conquered province of Kalinga was to be governed. You will stay here this night.

DAY 13:Jaugada (Ganjam) – Odisha – Bhubaneshwar – Odisha

You will leave to Bhubaneshwar after dinner

You will be dropped at the airport for your return journey. Time to say Good BYE.
