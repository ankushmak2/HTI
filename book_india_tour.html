Holi in India

Holi is one of the major festival of India and it is also known as festival of Colours which celebrated right after winters. Holi waves goodbye to winter and welcomes of spring in a rainbow of colours. In India it’s predominantly celebrated in the northern India. Basically it is celebrated in the month of March-but on different dates and the dates vary every year due to the fact that the Hindu calendar is based on solar cycles.

This is a colorful festival celebrated with much joy and fervor all over northern India. The most famous Holi is played in Vrindavan-Mathura, Jaipur and other parts of Norther India, Holi festival has an ancient origin and celebrates the triumph of ‘good’ over ‘bad’. The colorful festival bridges the social gap and renew sweet relationships. On this day, people hug and wish each other ‘Happy Holi’. Undoubtedly, this fascinating festival is celebrated in all over India but in North India the way of celebration is more spectacular and majestic. Detailed Tour Itinerary

DAY 01 : : Arrive Delhi
Welcome to India! You are about to embark upon a magical journey through the lands ruled by great emperors! On your trip, you would enjoy the hospitality, grandeur, art, culture and cuisine of this great nation. Buckle your seat belts, sit back and enjoy!

Delhi

is a bustling metropolis and the capital region of India, which includes the national capital city, New Delhi. The area around Delhi was probably inhabited before the second millennium BC, and there is evidence of continuous habitation since at least the 6th century BC. The city is believed to be the site of Indraprastha, the legendary capital of the Pandavas in the Indian epic Mahabharata. Through most of its history, Delhi has served as a capital of various kingdoms and empires. It has been captured, sacked and rebuilt several times, particularly during the medieval period, and therefore the modern conurbation of Delhi is a cluster of a number of cities spread across the metropolitan region.
Upon your arrival at Delhi International Airport, one of our team will meet you at Airport and after a warm welcome transfer you to hotel. If time permit you can explore the Delhi Sightseeing and Your evening is free for leisure at the Hotel (TBA).
DAY 02:: Delhi Sightseeing
Morning after breakfast drive Haridwar - located in the beautiful state of Uttranchal. This city is well known as Holy city of India. Upon your arrival check in hotel & Evening attend the Aarti Ceremony at Har ki Pauri Ghats, the most spiritual aarti for every Indian. Evening free for leisure and overnight at hotel.
DAY 03:Delhi – Mathura – Agra
After breakfast, proceed for an excursion trip of Rishikesh, Rishikesh is a holy city for Hindus located in the foothills of the Himalaya in northern India. Day free for Meditation and Yoga, overnight in Hotel.
DAY 04:Agra – Fatehpur Sikri – Jaipur
Morning after breakfast drive back to Delhi, on arrival transfer & Overnight in Hotel.
DAY 05:Jaipur
Morning after breakfast straight drive towards the Agra, On arrival isit the Agra Fort, taken over from the Lodhis by the Mughal Emperor Akbar in the late 16th century. The Fort can be more accurately described as a walled palatial city. It was built from red sandstone, often inlaid with white marble & intricate decorations. Then we will visit Sikandara, Akbar's tomb. His son Jahangir completed the construction of the tomb in the year 1613 and Evening sunset-view of the famous Mausoleum – The Taj Mahal, a unique epitome of Love built by Mughal Emperor Shahjahan in 1630 for his queen Mumtaz Mahal to enshrine her mortal remains. A drop of tear on the cheek of Time has now been crystallized! After exploring the beauty of Taj Mahal transfer to hotel, Evening free for leisure and Overnight at hotel..
DAY 06:Jaipur – Rohetgarh
Morning after breakfast, straight drive towards the Pink City – Jaipur, Enroute you will visit the city of Mughal Emperor Akbar Fatehpur Sikri (40 KMS from Agra) built in 1569 and abandoned it just after 15 years due to water scarcity. Visit the graceful buildings – The Punch Mahal, Anup Talab,Diwan-e- Khas, Tomb of the holy saint Salim Chisti, Buland Darwaza and other important palaces where one experiences a fusion of different architectures. After Fatehpur Sikri, also continue drive towards Jaipur, Enroute we will visit the Abhaneri, Step wells one of the larger wells in India, earlier which was used for Water Storage, on arrival evening free for leisure and night at Hotel.
DAY 07:Rohetgarh - Ranakpur – Udaipur
Morning after breakfast at 730hrs visit to Amber Fort, the ancient Capital of the state until 1728. Enjoy the Elephant ride up to the hill on which the Fort is situated. And do the ultimate Photography while climbing the fort on Elephants. Visit the Jagmandir or the hall of victory, Jaimahal (Look at the beautiful mirror work and intricate inlay work)

Later city sightseeing tour visiting Maharaja’s City Palace and the Observatory. Drive pass Hawa Mahal or the Palace of winds through the rose – pink residential and business areas of Jaipur and time to photography and also visit the water palace and do the street photography. Evening free for leisure and overnight at hotel.
DAY 08:Udaipur
Morning after breakfast option to go for Monkey Temple or Galta Ji Temple – a local temple of Jaipur and here you have option to feed the monkeys with Bread & Bananas
Later drive back to Delhi, on arrival Evening free for leisure and overnight in Hotel.
DAY 09:Udaipur – Delhi
Upon your arrival on Cochin airport from your home state, after a warm welcome pick up from Airport and transferred to your hotel and later in the afternoon enjoy a half day sightseeing of this colonial port town, which is famous for its Mattanchery Dutch Palace (closed on Friday's), Jewish Synagogue (closed on Friday's, Saturday's and Jewish Holiday's), Jew Street, old Churches and huge Chinese fishing nets, which adorn the northern shores. Evening watch the dance drama of Kerala – Kathakali show in a local theatre. Overnight at Hotel.
DAY 10:Delhi – Departure.
Morning after breakfast drive to Munnar (about 145 km /04 hrs), a picturesque hill station covered with unending expanses of tea plantations. Arrive and check into your hotel and later in the afternoon enjoy a half day sightseeing, which consists of Eravikulam National Park, which houses the endangered species Nilgiri Ibex. Explore the park on foot; the views are stunning toward the tea capped peaks. O/n Hotel.
