Detailed Tour Itinerary

DAY 01 :ARRIVAL KATHMANDU
Welcome to Kathmandu! Upon arrival in Tribhuvan International airport, Kathmandu You will be met by our representatives who will provide transportation to your hotel. No activities are planned for the day. You are free to explore picturesque down town Kathmandu. On your request, we will be happy to, accompany you for more activities.
DAY 02:FULL DAY SIGHTSEEING TOUR
After breakfast, we start for a full day sightseeing tour. The places of today’s visit will be Pashupatinath temple, Boudhanath stupa and Bhaktapur city.

Pashupati:

is the most important Hindu temple in Nepal and this is one of the most important Shiva temples on the subcontinent and draws many devotees from all over India too. Pashupati is considered to have a special concern for Nepal. Pashupatinath, an ancient base, by the banks of the holy Bagmati River, stands the 16th century of Pashupatinath-Shiva, as Lord of all Creatures. The Old buildings on the site were destroyed by Moslem invaders in the 14th century, its stone linga smashed to dust, but it rose again behind their retreating backs. Succeeding monarchs reinstated the temple and the images. The following Bagmati is a holy river and the ghats (crematorium) at its bank are the earnest Hindus truest cremation center.

Boudhanath:

Boudhanath is the largest stupa among in South Asia, and it has become the central point of Tibetan Buddhism in Nepal. The white mound looms thirty-six meters overhead. This ancient stupa, one of the biggest in the world, has the all-seeing eyes of Lord Buddha. It was built in the fifth century on an octagonal base inset with prayer wheels.

Bhaktapur:

(city of devotees) covers an area of four square miles. Bhaktapur still retains a medieval charm and visitors to this ancient town are treated to numerous wonders of cultural and artistic achievements. Pottery and waving are its traditional industries. Bhaktapur is famous for woodcarving and the traditional topi (cap). The city located about 14km east of Kathmandu. The places of interest include Bhaktapur Durbar Square, the National Art Gallery, Nyatapola Temple, Bhairavnath temple, Dattatreya temple and Dattatreya Square. Bhaktapur Durbar Square contains innumerable temples and other architectural showpieces such as lion gate, the golden gate, the palace of 55 windows being built by king Yaksha Malla in 1927 AD., The Nyatapola temple this five storied Pogada Style temple is the tallest pagoda structure in the Kathmandu valley.
DAY 03:FULL DAY SIGHTSEEING TOUR
After breakfast, we start for a full day sightseeing tour. The places of today’s visit will be Kathmandu Durbar Square, Monkey Temple (Swyambhunath stupa) and Patan city.

Kathmandu Durbar Square:

This complex of palaces, courtyards and temples, built between the 12th and 18th centuries used to be the seat of Malla kings of Kathmandu. The Durbar square, protected as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the social religious and urban focal point of the city.There are also several Museums inside the palace building.

Monkey temple (Swayambhunath stupa):

This is considered as the oldest Buddhist stupa. It watches over the valley on its western side from the top of a hillock. The stupa is one of the holiest Buddhist sites of Nepal. It’ establishment is linked to the creation of Kathmandu valley out of a primordial lake. It’s listed in World Heritage site.

Patan Durbar Square:

This Durbar Square like its counter part in Kathmandu is an enchanting mélange of palace building, artistic courtyards and graceful pagoda temples. The former Royal place complex is the center of Patan’s religious and social life and houses a museum containing an array of bronze statues and religious objects. One remarkable monument here is a 17th built entirely of stone. It is listed on World Heritage site.-century temple dedicated to the Hindu God Krishna
DAY 04:MOUNTAIN FLIGHT AND HALF DAY SIGHTSEEING TOUR
Early in the morning transfer to airport for Mountain flight to have the magnificent view of highestmountains on earth including Mt. Everest. After mountain flight transfer to hotel have rest. In the afternoon start half day sightseeing tour of Changu Narayan temple.

Changunarayan

is a beautiful and historic temple stands on a hilltop at the eastern end of the valley, about 5 km from Bhaktapur. Although the temple dates from 1702 when it was rebuilt after a fire, its origins go right back to 4th century, and there are many important stone images and sculptures dating from the Licchavvi period.
DAY 05:DRIVE LUMBINI
After breakfast drive 232 km/145 miles away from Kathmandu. Lumbini is the birth place of Lord Buddha. The broken Ashoka Pillar, remnants of old monastery, images of Buddha's mother Maya Devi, is still preserved here. The Buddha himself proclaimed Lumbini as one of the foremost pilgrimage sites among the famous Chatumahasthana (the four great places of Birth, Enlightenment, Sermon and Mahaparinirvana).
Lumbini hosts variety of religious groups of people. It is home of Hindus,Buddhists, Muslims and Christians. The Lumbini area is located in southwest of Rupandehi. The population of Lumbini is segmented in several cultural communities. The major cultures here are Bhojpuri and Tharu.
The major cultural sub groups are Biraha, Tharu, Jhumada and segmented Bhojpuri culture as well. The cultural communities follow their own tradition of costume, make up, entertainment performance and also their own language.
DAY 06:SIGHTSEENG IN LUMBINI
Sightseeing of Lumbini includes a visit to the sacred garden, Maya Devi Temple, holy pond and monasteries built within the premises of the Garden. Different Buddhist countries from all over the world have built these.
The main attraction is the Sacred Garden spread over 8sq km and possessing all the treasure of the historic area. The Sacred Garden where Buddha was born converges on the Ashoka pillar, which carries an inscription identifying the spot as the birthplace of Buddha. Maya Devi, mother of Buddha, was on her way to her parent's home in Devadaha. While taking rest in Lumbini under a Sal tree and admiring the beauty of the region, she felt labor pains. She gave birth to the child while holding a drooping branch of a Sal tree.
The Mayadevi temple is the main attraction for pilgrims. The temple of Maya Devi contains a stone relief depicting the birth scene of Lord Buddha. The bas-relief shows Maya Devi supporting herself by holding on to a branch of a sal tree, and the newborn infant Buddha standing upright on a lotus pedestal. Two celestial figures are engaged in the act of pouring water and lotuses from the heaven, indicated in the sculpture by a delineation of clouds. The Maya Devi shrine has been worshipped by both Hindus and Buddhists since the beginning of the Christian era and is believed to have been built over the foundation of at least one earlier temple or stupa.
DAY 07:DRIVE CHITWAN
In the morning you will be driven to Chitwan National Park a wildlife reserve, where you will spend the night. Briefing on Jungle program by guide and after lunch Tharu village walk, visit Museum and sun set tour. Evening enjoy Tharu stick dance and Dinner at Resort.

Village tour:

This tour is especially for those who are keen to experience typical local culture of the Tharus.

Ox-cart ride:

For those who love old ethnic traditional way of transport, we have an oxcart that will give you an experience of the unique mode of transport while riding on the old dirt road of the nearby Tharu village.

Tharu folk dance:

At the end of the day you will enjoy the drum beats and the agile breathtaking stick dance performed by the Tharus. Their rhythmic movements and the exciting drum beats may urge you to join in their performance.

Chitwan

Chitwan is one of the finest national parks in Asia, renowned for its dense concentration of wildlife and renowned for its dense concentration of wildlife and top class tourist lodges, which provide the opportunity to see animals in their natural habitat. Chitwan which means the ‘heart of the jungle’ is among the last surviving examples of the continuous band of forests and grassland which one extended from the Indus River in Pakistan to the Burmese border. The main attraction here is the Royal Chitwan National Park, one of the largest forest regions in Asia teeming with one horned rhinoceros, several species of deer, sloth bear, leopard, wild boar, crocodile, more than 350 species of birds and the exclusive Royal Bengal Tiger. Royal Chitwan National park is listed in the UNESCO World Heritage Site (natural) list. The five hour drive to Chitwan (165 km) overland is filled with thrilling views of the hills, rivers and plains with jungles one either side.

DAY 08:WILDLIFE ACTIVITIES IN CHITWAN
  • Wakeup call
  • Tea / Coffee followed by Nature walk for Bird watching
  • Breakfast
  • Canoe ride along with Elephant breeding center
  • Lunch
  • Visit to National Park visitor center (Information about the Park)
  • Elephant safari (Approx. 2 hours)
  • Slides presentation about the flora & fauna of the National Park
  • Dinner


Canoe ride:

A typical home made canoe will take you along the famous Rapti river where you will see the harmless Gharial and or the mud drenched crocodile basking in the sun at the river banks. Many aquatic birds can also be seen on the way.

Nature walk:

Our trained naturalist will take you across the flowing water of Rapti river and the prime areas of the park to view nature's gift the wild species of animal and birds blended with the surrounding flora and fauna.

Bird-watching:

The park is well known as a paradise for the birds. Some 450 species of resident and migratory birds have been recorded so far. You may encounter too many of them, while making this tour. Other species like the Giant Hornbill, Lesser Frolic and the Paradise Flycatcher will also attract your attention when you are traveling along the banks of the Rapti River.

Elephant back safari:

Riding Elephant Back is one of the best ways to explore the jungle. The tour enables you to enter into the dense forest and tall grassland to view varieties of animal species and birds. Here, you will have plenty of opportunities to photograph the animals from a close range.
DAY 09:DRIVE KATHMANDU
After breakfast drive back to Kathmandu. You are free the whole day for relaxation or for other individual activities.
DAY 10:FINAL DEPARTURE
Depending on your flight time, we will drive you to the International airport for your onward flight.
